Each alternative, honestly

Plutus — lower fees + crypto

5% flat, native Stripe + NOWPayments crypto, per-product themed pages, instant crypto payouts. Best if you drive your own traffic and want to keep more of each sale. Weak spot: no native subscriptions (yet).

Lemon Squeezy — tax-handled MoR

Acts as merchant of record — they handle VAT and sales tax globally. ~5% + processing. Best if you sell to EU/UK and hate tax compliance. Not the cheapest, but worth it for tax peace of mind at scale.

Podia — all-in-one

Downloads, courses, coaching, memberships, email in one. $39–$89/month. Best if you sell multiple product types and want less stack. More expensive at low volume; cheap at high volume.

Payhip — simple + free tier

Free tier at 5% fee, $29/month to drop to 2% fee. Basic product pages, decent features. Good entry-level option with room to grow.

Stan Store — bio + shop

$29–$99/month, 0% platform fee. A mobile-first creator bio page with product sales attached. Best for Instagram/TikTok creators where the bio is the sales surface.

Sellfy — mature classic

$29–$159/month. Full-featured digital + physical product seller. Been around forever. Good if you want mature, predictable, full-featured.

Ko-fi — tipping-first

Free, 0% on direct sales (5% on Ko-fi Shop). Best for creators who want tipping + occasional digital sales + light paid memberships. Limits on scaling to serious product businesses.

FAQ

Good questions

Why is Gumroad's 10% fee a problem?
At $5,000/month in sales, Gumroad's fee is $500/month — $6,000/year. Cutting it to 5% saves half of that, which pays for a lot of your content production, hosting, and tools.
Can I use multiple platforms simultaneously?
Yes. Many creators keep Gumroad for one product and use Plutus (or another) for another. Biggest downside: fragmented analytics. For unified reporting, pick one primary.
Which is easiest to migrate to from Gumroad?
All of them are easy to migrate to — you recreate products manually (15 min each for 5–10 products). The friction is not technical, it is getting your audience to use the new URL.
Which is best for a creator making under $500/month?
Plutus, Payhip free, or Ko-fi — all have no monthly fee, and they scale with your revenue. Avoid monthly-subscription platforms until you are doing $2k+/month consistently.
Which is best for international tax handling?
Lemon Squeezy, hands down. They are a merchant of record that handles VAT, GST, and sales tax globally. For creators selling 25%+ to EU/UK, the extra fee pays for itself in compliance savings.
Does Gumroad have any advantages left?
Brand recognition and Discover traffic (small but non-zero). If you have zero audience, Gumroad's built-in marketplace may drive a few extra sales. If you have any audience, the 10% fee is harder to justify.
