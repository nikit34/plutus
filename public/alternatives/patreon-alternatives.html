Rethinking the model

Do you actually need a subscription?

Patreon is built for monthly patronage — $5 × 200 patrons = $1,000/month recurring. That is beautiful if you post regularly and your audience wants ongoing access.

But many creators who try Patreon find the monthly cadence obligation draining, and a smaller subset of patrons would happily pay $99 once for a "greatest hits" archive than $5/month forever.

Before picking a Patreon alternative, ask: am I actually selling monthly access, or am I selling one-time products? The answer changes which of the platforms below is right.

The alternatives

Eight Patreon-alternative approaches

Plutus — one-time products

Different model: one-time digital product sales, not subscriptions. 5% flat fee, crypto checkout, instant payouts. Best if your "Patreon tier" is really just "a thing I sell to people."

Substack — newsletter subscriptions

10% platform fee + Stripe. Subscription-first, built around writing. Best for creators whose output is written (essays, analysis, fiction). Built-in discovery through the Substack network.

Ko-fi — tips + small memberships

0% on direct / 5% on Ko-fi Gold membership system. Best for creators with smaller audiences who want lightweight tipping + occasional paid extras. Minimal Bureaucracy.

Buy Me a Coffee — tips + products

5% on memberships, 0% on tips, 5% on shop. Cute, mobile-first interface. Best if your audience is casual and you want a low-commitment support option.

Memberful — membership software

~5% + $19–$129/month. Self-hosted membership software that runs on your own site. Best if you already have a site and want full control over the member experience.

Circle — community platform

$39–$149/month. Communities-first with gated content, events, and posts. Best if your core offer is a community, not a content feed.

Gumroad — one-time products

10% flat fee. Similar to Plutus in model (one-time sales) but with higher fees and older brand presence. Decent default but not the lowest-fee option.

Ghost — newsletter + paid

$9–$199/month self-hosted. Publication platform with paid subscriptions. Best if you want full ownership and control over your publishing infrastructure.

Picking by use case

The decision tree

You sell courses, PDFs, templates, digital products: Plutus or Gumroad. One-time model fits better than subscription.

You write essays / analysis / newsletter: Substack, Ghost, or Beehiiv. Subscription model works here.

You have a community as the main offer: Circle or Mighty Networks. Community-first platforms beat Patreon for community-first creators.

You want tips + occasional products + casual vibes: Ko-fi or Buy Me a Coffee.

You already have a website and want membership on it: Memberful or Ghost self-hosted.

You sell crypto-related content or have a crypto-native audience: Plutus is the only one with native crypto checkout + payouts.

The hybrid play

Running two tools in parallel

Many creators find the best revenue comes from stacking models: a small monthly subscription (Patreon, Substack, Ko-fi Gold) for recurring income, plus occasional one-time product launches (Plutus) for revenue spikes.

The monthly base smooths the revenue curve. The product launches generate the big months that fund bigger projects. Neither tool fully replaces the other — they solve different halves of the creator economics.

FAQ

Good questions

Is Patreon still worth using in 2026?
Yes, if your product is monthly patronage with tier-gated posts. For one-time products, a creator at any volume pays less with Plutus, Gumroad, or Lemon Squeezy.
Which has the lowest fees?
Plutus at 5% flat is the lowest among full-featured platforms. Ko-fi has 0% on direct tips but 5% on Gold memberships and shop sales. Buy Me a Coffee also 0% on tips.
Can I switch from Patreon without losing my patrons?
Not automatically — Patreon won't port subscription billing for you. What you can do: create your new platform, announce to patrons with a 60-day transition window, offer a discount for early movers. Expect 40–60% port rate on the first try.
Does Plutus handle recurring subscriptions?
Not as a first-class feature today. For recurring memberships, Patreon, Substack, or Circle are better fits. Plutus excels at the one-time-product half of creator economics.
Which is best for writers?
Substack or Ghost. They are purpose-built for written content with built-in newsletter delivery. Patreon's feed is less optimized for long-form writing.
Can I promote my Plutus products on Patreon?
Yes — nothing prevents you from having a Patreon tier that includes a discount code for your Plutus products. Many creators run both and cross-promote.
