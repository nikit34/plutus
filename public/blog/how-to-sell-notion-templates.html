How to sell Notion templates (actually working playbook)

Not another "just list it on Gumroad" post. This is the step-by-step process creators use to go from zero to $1,000/mo in Notion template sales — covering what to build, how to price, and how to drive the first 100 buyers.

Step 1

Pick a niche, not a template

The biggest mistake first-time template creators make is starting with "I'll build a productivity template" — a product without an audience. The winning approach is to pick an audience first, then a workflow that audience runs on.

Good niches: freelance writers tracking clients and invoices; newsletter authors planning 12 issues ahead; Etsy sellers managing SKUs and ad spend; second-year PhD students managing citations and reading queues.

Bad niches: "productivity", "life OS", "second brain". Too broad, no one specific who urgently needs it.

A useful filter: can you name three real people in that niche who would pay money tomorrow? If not, keep narrowing.

Step 2

Build like a product, not a playground

A Notion template that sells has three things a free one does not: real sample data, a 60-second onboarding video, and a written "start here" page inside the template itself.

Sample data matters more than people think. A freelancer-invoicing template with "Example Invoice #001 — Acme Co — $2,400" beats an empty table every time. Buyers can see the shape of their future workflow without imagining it.

Record the demo with Loom or CleanShot and embed it on the product page. Not the template itself — on the product page, before purchase. It converts like nothing else.

Step 3

Price with anchors

Solo creators systematically under-price. A three-tier ladder ($19 / $49 / $149) outsells a single-tier offer 2–3x because the $49 middle tier looks like a deal against the $149 agency tier.

Light tier ($9–$19): core workflow, personal use.

Standard tier ($29–$49): everything in light + automations, templates-within-the-template, email support.

Agency tier ($99–$149): everything + white-label license, resale rights to clients, onboarding call.

On Plutus, you set three products with the same cover and a visible "which tier is right for you?" comparison. Most revenue flows through the middle tier — the other two exist to anchor.

Step 4

Set up your store in five minutes

  1. Upload the Notion duplicate link. On Plutus, create a new product and paste the Notion URL. Add the cover, set the price, pick a theme. Done in one screen.
  2. Enable both payment rails. Card checkout (Stripe) for domestic buyers, crypto checkout (NOWPayments, USDT/BTC/ETH) for international. Both on by default.
  3. Share the product URL. You get plutus.firstmessage.ru/product/your-slug. Drop it in your Twitter bio, Telegram channel, YouTube description. One link, everywhere.
Step 5

The first 100 buyers

You will not sell a template by posting "I made a template, please buy" once and walking away. The creators who hit $1,000/mo in their first quarter do four things weekly:

1. Twitter threads. One thread per week showing the template solving a specific problem. Not features — outcomes. "How I tracked 47 client invoices in 20 minutes a week."

2. Telegram / Discord presence. Share tips and excerpts in communities where your niche lives. Three helpful posts for every one sales post.

3. YouTube walk-through. A 10-minute video of you using the template on a real example. This carries for 12+ months because YouTube indexes it.

4. Email. Start a simple newsletter (Beehiiv or ConvertKit). Every buyer joins. Every Friday, a short note with one template tip. Email is where repeat purchases happen.

FAQ

Good questions

How much can you realistically make selling Notion templates?
Most creators who stick with it hit $500–$2,000/mo within the first six months if they ship weekly and pick a real niche. A small number scale past $10,000/mo with a ladder of products, an audience of 10k+ engaged followers, and active community presence. The ceiling is meaningfully higher than most people expect — but it requires consistency, not talent.
Should I sell on Gumroad or Plutus or both?
For first-time sellers, pick one and focus. Plutus has lower fees (5% vs 10%), native crypto support for international buyers, and cleaner product pages. If you already have a Gumroad audience, you can run both briefly and migrate once your Plutus traffic matches.
Do I need my own website?
No. Both Plutus and Gumroad host your product page. Use your bio link as the call-to-action. A blog or landing page helps later but is not required to start.
How do I protect my template from being shared?
You cannot perfectly. A Notion duplicate link can be re-shared. Two mitigations: (1) use signed, per-buyer delivery links (Plutus does this by default) so public URLs are not trivially exposed, (2) ship enough value via your community and updates that customers see paying as worth it anyway.
Which categories sell best in 2026?
Client portals for freelancers and small agencies, newsletter/content planning systems, personal finance trackers with tax integration, and niche-specific CRMs (real estate, coaching, fitness) consistently outsell generic "productivity" templates.
How fast can I go live?
From zero to first product live on Plutus: about 10 minutes. From zero to first sale: typically 1–3 weeks if you have any audience, 2–3 months if you're building one from scratch.
