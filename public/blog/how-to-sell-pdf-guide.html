How to sell a PDF guide (practical playbook)

A working process for turning your expertise into a PDF that sells. What to write about, how to price, how to package, and where to put it so people actually buy — not just download.

Step 1

Write the PDF people would pay for

A sellable PDF is not a long blog post. It is a compact, problem-solving deliverable that saves the reader time or money. Before you write a word, answer three questions: who specifically is reading this, what exactly do they walk away knowing/having, and what would they google if they did not have your guide.

Target 20–40 pages. Longer guides do not sell better — they actually convert worse because they feel overwhelming. Tight, practical, specific beats comprehensive.

Step 2

Structure that converts

Page 1: Problem. Describe the pain in specific terms, so the reader thinks "yes, this is exactly me."

Page 2: Promise. Tell them exactly what they will know or have by the end.

Pages 3–25: Meat. Steps, frameworks, templates, real examples. Screenshots. No filler.

Page 26: Worksheet / Template. Something they can fill in or customize. Increases perceived value.

Page 27: What's next. Upsell to your course, consultation, or bigger offer if you have one.

Step 3

Design without being a designer

Use Canva, Figma, or Notion → PDF export. Pick one sans-serif font, one accent color, and stick with them for all 30 pages. Consistency matters more than cleverness.

Cover matters. Spend an hour on a single cover image that looks like a book, not a tweet. If design is not your strength, hire on Fiverr for $20–$50 for a cover + layout pass.

Include at least 3–5 screenshots, diagrams, or example snippets. Visual breaks every 2–3 pages keep readers engaged.

Step 4

Price based on outcome, not page count

$9–$19: Quick reference, cheat sheet, recipe-book style. Entry-level, high-volume.

$29–$49: Frameworks + templates + case studies. The sweet spot for most expert guides.

$79–$149: Premium guides with worksheets, video addons, and a private Discord/Telegram invite. Stacks well.

Pages do not price a PDF. Outcome does. A 15-page guide on "How to write a book proposal that gets a $50k advance" prices higher than a 100-page general writing guide.

Step 5

Launch in under an hour

  1. Export your PDF. From Canva/Figma/Notion. Keep file size under 25MB — large PDFs slow the buy experience and some email clients block them.
  2. Create the Plutus product. Upload the PDF. Set title, description (3 short paragraphs: problem, promise, what's inside), cover image. Pick a price. Enable card + crypto checkout.
  3. Get one clean URL. You get plutus.firstmessage.ru/product/your-slug. That is the only URL you need. Post it.
Step 6

Get your first 50 buyers

Week 1: Your existing audience. Newsletter, Twitter thread, Telegram channel, LinkedIn post. If you have 500+ followers who know you, 1–5% will buy something they have been waiting for — that is 5–25 immediate sales.

Week 2: The writeup thread. Twitter/Threads: take the best 5 concepts from your PDF and turn them into a 10-tweet thread. End with "this is one section from my $X guide — full thing here: [link]." This one pattern has sold more PDFs than any other single tactic.

Week 3–4: Repurpose and spread. YouTube short, LinkedIn carousel, Reddit answer, Instagram reel. One guide supports 20+ pieces of derivative content over a month.

FAQ

Good questions

What tools should I write the PDF in?
Canva for design-first PDFs, Google Docs for text-heavy guides (export as PDF), Figma for highly visual layouts, Notion for structured frameworks. Pick the one you are fastest in.
How long should a sellable PDF be?
20–40 pages is the sweet spot. Under 15 feels too thin. Over 50 feels like homework and converts worse.
How do I stop my PDF from being shared for free?
You cannot perfectly. Per-buyer signed delivery (Plutus default) prevents public URL sharing. For determined sharing, accept that a small fraction leaks; your first-buyer conversion rate is what pays the bills anyway.
Should I offer a preview / free chapter?
Yes — a free 2-3 page excerpt on your newsletter signup raises buy-rate 15–30%. Also drives email list growth.
What about selling in multiple currencies?
Plutus card checkout supports 30+ currencies via Stripe. Crypto checkout settles in USD. Both work from any country.
How much can I realistically make from one PDF?
Depends on audience size and niche. $29 × 100 buyers = $2,900. Well-placed PDF launches for creators with 10k+ engaged followers routinely do $3k–$10k in the first month. Long tail drives 30–50% of total lifetime revenue.
