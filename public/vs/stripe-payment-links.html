Plutus vs Stripe Payment Links

What Stripe Payment Links give you

A checkout URL — and that is it

Stripe Payment Links are an elegant primitive: create a link, share it, collect payment. No monthly fee, no platform cut (just Stripe's standard processing: 2.9% + $0.30).

For a single-product freelancer, or for collecting deposits on invoices, that is all you need. The challenge begins when your product is a digital file, a course, or anything that requires delivery after payment.

Where Payment Links end

What you build yourself when using Stripe alone

Delivery mechanism. After payment, you need to send the buyer the file, link, or access. With Payment Links alone, this is a manual email, a webhook + Zapier setup, or custom code.

Per-buyer license protection. If you share a public download URL, it leaks within days. You need signed, time-limited URLs per buyer. This is a custom backend.

Product page. Payment Links generate a Stripe-hosted page that is functional but bare. For an illustrator or course creator, the buying experience starts before the checkout — your product page matters.

Analytics. Stripe shows transactions. It does not show conversion rate, revenue-per-view, or which traffic source converted. You need that to scale.

Crypto checkout. Stripe does not accept crypto directly. If you want USDT/BTC buyers, you integrate NOWPayments/Coinbase Commerce yourself.

Refunds dashboard, receipts, notifications. Stripe has them for payments; for digital-product-specific flows (re-send download link, extend expiration), you build or skip.

The trade

What 5% is actually buying

The 5% Plutus fee is the difference between "DIY Stripe + 5 tools + custom code" and "paste a link, ship product, get paid." For most indie creators, the cost of their time solving delivery, per-buyer licensing, analytics, and crypto checkout far exceeds 5% of revenue.

Do the math: if your course costs $200 and you sell 10 copies, Plutus' fee is $100. Solving signed delivery and a per-buyer license system in code costs many hours; solving analytics and a better product page, many more. At $10/hour value of your time, Plutus pays for itself on the first sale.

Decision

When each is the right call

  1. Use Stripe Payment Links when. You are a freelancer invoicing for services. You are collecting a one-off deposit. You already have a custom site handling delivery, analytics, and branding.
  2. Use Plutus when. You are selling digital products (templates, courses, PDFs) to multiple buyers. You want per-buyer delivery, analytics, crypto, and a branded page without coding any of it.
  3. Use both when. Offer products on Plutus; use Payment Links for bespoke quotes or service invoicing. Nothing stops you.
FAQ

Good questions

Do I still use Stripe with Plutus?
Yes — Plutus uses Stripe Connect for card processing. Your Stripe account handles the money; Plutus adds the product-platform layer. Payouts land in the same Stripe-connected bank account.
Can I bring my own Stripe Payment Link into Plutus?
Yes — Plutus supports a "custom payment link" field on each product. If you want Stripe to host the checkout (maybe you have a specific setup), point Plutus at your existing Payment Link.
Is Plutus cheaper than building Stripe + custom code?
Depends on your hourly rate and how many products you sell. For a single product with <50 sales, building yourself is cheaper. For 5+ products with 100+ sales, Plutus usually wins on total cost.
Does Plutus replace Stripe?
No — Plutus runs on Stripe. Think of Plutus as "Stripe Payment Links plus everything you would otherwise build" for digital-product sellers.
What about refunds?
Plutus refunds go through Stripe (or NOWPayments for crypto). One-click from your Wallet dashboard. Full or partial refunds supported.
