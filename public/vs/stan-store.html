Two different tools

Link-in-bio ≠ product platform

Stan Store started as Linktree for creators — a mobile-first landing page with multiple links and, later, paid digital products baked in. Its strength is the unified "everything you do" creator bio page.

Plutus is product-first. Each product gets a dedicated, themed page optimized for conversion of that specific thing. You can absolutely share a Plutus URL from Linktree or an Instagram bio, but Plutus itself is not a bio page.

Cost breakdown

The monthly-fee question

Stan Store charges $29/month for the Starter plan and $99/month for Pro. That is $348–$1,188 per year in fixed cost before you sell anything.

Plutus has no monthly fee. You pay 5% only on actual sales. At $1,000/month in revenue, you pay $50 — less than the Stan Store monthly on any tier. At $5,000/month, Plutus is ~$250 vs Stan's $29–$99 depending on tier; Stan becomes cheaper at scale if you hit the volume that matches their flat pricing.

For most creators under $2,000/month in product revenue, Plutus is cheaper. For creators reliably doing $5,000+/month, Stan Store's flat monthly can be competitive.

Page quality

Per-product pages vs bio pages

The design choice is philosophical. A bio page (Stan Store) serves many purposes — drive to YouTube, show latest post, sell product, collect newsletter — and does each OK. A product page (Plutus) does one thing: convert a visitor to a buyer of that specific product.

If your traffic is cold (Instagram follower who barely knows you), a bio page helps them orient first. If your traffic is warm (newsletter link, Twitter thread about the specific product), a dedicated product page converts 2–3x better.

The hybrid play: use Linktree, Stan, or a bio tool for your main bio page; link out to Plutus URLs for individual products. Best of both.

Decision

Which to pick

  1. Pick Stan Store if. You want one unified bio-and-store page, you do <$3k/mo in digital product sales, and your audience traffic is mostly cold/exploring.
  2. Pick Plutus if. You sell specific products to warm audiences, you want crypto rails, or you want dedicated high-converting pages per product.
  3. Do both. Many creators keep a bio tool (Linktree/Stan) as their main link, and use Plutus URLs for specific product CTAs inside newsletters, threads, and YouTube descriptions.
FAQ

Good questions

Can I use Plutus as my link in bio?
You can link to your best-converting Plutus product page from your Instagram bio. It will not serve as a multi-link page, so if you also want YouTube / newsletter / other links, pair Plutus with Linktree, Beacons, or a personal landing page.
Does Plutus have a "shop" page aggregating my products?
Not as a customer-facing feature today. Each product lives at its own URL. For a product catalog view, link your personal site or bio tool to individual Plutus products.
What about Stan Store's coaching / booking features?
Plutus handles booking products via text delivery (include your Cal.com/Calendly link as the deliverable). Stan Store has it as a first-class feature. If booking is a major part of your offer, Stan may fit better.
Does Stan Store accept crypto?
Not natively. If your audience is crypto-native or international, Plutus' native USDT/BTC checkout is a meaningful advantage — especially for Web3, NFT-adjacent, and non-US/EU creators.
Can I migrate from Stan Store to Plutus?
Yes — recreate your products on Plutus, point your traffic to the new URLs, keep Stan Store running while you transition if you want. Export your customer list from Stan first.
